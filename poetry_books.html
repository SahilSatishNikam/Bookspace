Shakespers Sonnets
The Arden Shakespeare has long been acclaimed as the established scholarly edition of Shakespeare's work. Now being totally reedited for the third time, Arden editions offer the very best in contemporary scholarship. Each volume provides a clear and authoritative text, edited to the highest standards; detailed textual notes and commentary on the same page of the text; full contextual, illustrated introduction, including an in-depth survey of critical and performance approaches to the play; and selected bibliography.



The Raven And Other Poems
The Raven and Other Poems is a poetry collection by Edgar Allan Poe, published in 1845 by Wiley and Putnam. It includes Poe's famous poem *“The Raven”*, which brought him literary fame that same year. The collection also features Lenore, To Helen, Israfel, and "The Haunted Palace." Themes include death, lost love, madness, the supernatural, and beauty. Poe's style is known for musical rhythm, internal rhyme, and dark, gothic imagery. This book helped establish Poe as a major figure in American Romantic and Gothic literature.

The Road Not Taken
The Road Not Taken is a famous poem by Robert Frost, first published in 1915 in The Atlantic Monthly*. It was later included in his 1916 poetry collection *Mountain Interval. The poem explores themes of choice, uncertainty, and the consequences of decisions in life. Structured in four stanzas of five lines each, it uses a rhyme scheme of ABAAB. Though often seen as promoting individualism, it subtly highlights the ambiguity of choices. It remains one of the most quoted and studied poems in American literature.

Les Fleurs du mal
Les Fleurs du mal is a poetry collection by Charles Baudelaire, first published in 1857. It explores themes like beauty, sin, love, death, and the conflict between ideal and reality. The book faced legal action for immorality, with six poems banned until 1949. It is divided into sections such as *Spleen et Idéal*, *Le Vin*, and *La Mort*. Baudelaire's style blends rich symbolism, musicality, and dark, emotional imagery. The work greatly influenced Symbolist and Modernist poetry, marking a shift in French literature.

Poèmes saturniens
Poèmes saturniens is Paul Verlaine’s first major poetry collection, published in 1866. The poems explore themes of melancholy, love, nature, time, and existential reflection. Named after the planet Saturn, symbolizing gloom and sadness, the collection has a somber tone. Verlaine’s style combines musicality, traditional forms, and emerging Symbolist imagery. Notable poems include *“Chanson d’automne”* and *“Le ciel est, par-dessus le toit.”* This work established Verlaine as a leading figure in French poetry and influenced later Symbolists.

Collected Poems
Collected Poems by Dylan Thomas was first published in 1952, gathering his complete poetic works. The collection includes famous poems like “Do not go gentle into that good night” and “Fern Hill.” Themes often explore life, death, nature, time, memory, and mortality. Thomas is known for his lyrical, musical language and rich, vivid imagery. His poetry combines intense emotion with complex rhythms and inventive wordplay. This collection cemented Thomas’s place as a leading 20th-century Welsh poet and influenced many others.
