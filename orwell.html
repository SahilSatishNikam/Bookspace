George Orwell – Life, Works & Legacy

1.Early Life & Background

• Real name: Eric Arthur Blair, born 25 June 1903 in Motihari, Bengal, India. He adopted the pen name “George Orwell” after his first book for both anonymity and literary identity Encyclopedia BritannicaWikipedia.

• Educated in England at Eton College on scholarship, though he never attended university due to financial constraints WikipediaBiography.

• From 1922–1927, he served in the Indian Imperial Police in Burma. This experience deeply shaped his early anti-imperialist views and literary themes The Times of IndiaBiography.

2.Early Writing & Social Experiments After resigning in 1927, Orwell lived among the poor in Paris and London, working various menial jobs — including as a dishwasher — to better understand their plight. This period inspired his debut work Down and Out in Paris and London (1933) factsking.comBiography. He later became a teacher and continued writing essays and journalism, deepening his critique of British society factsking.comBiography.

3.Political Experiences & Non-Fiction Orwell fought in the Spanish Civil War (1936–1937) for the Republican side and was wounded — this became the basis for his memoir Homage to Catalonia (1938). The war reinforced his stance against totalitarianism in all forms WikipediaBiography. Other notable non-fiction works include The Road to Wigan Pier (1937), documenting working-class struggles in northern England WikipediaIndia Today.

4.Fictional Masterpieces Animal Farm (1945): A sharp allegorical novella satirizing Stalinism and tyranny, using farm animals to portray power structures turning corrupt WikipediaThe New Yorker. Nineteen Eighty-Four (1949): A dystopian novel depicting a totalitarian regime under constant surveillance by “Big Brother.” Introduced terms like "doublethink," "Newspeak," and "thoughtcrime" into global discourse WikipediaLe Monde.fr.

5.Essays & Language Orwell was a compelling essayist; his well-known essays—such as “Politics and the English Language”—warn about how language can be manipulated for political ends AblisonVanity Fair. His works continually blended personal narrative with broader social critique, offering a human voice amid political discourse The New YorkerVanity Fair.

6.Beliefs & Legacy Orwell was a committed democratic socialist, staunchly opposed to totalitarianism—whether fascist or Communist. He emphasized defending objective truth against authoritarian propaganda WikipediaLe Monde.fr+1. His vivid portrayals of surveillance, propaganda, and social control lend his name to the adjective “Orwellian”—used to describe oppressive regimes and manipulative political tactics WikipediaLe Monde.fr.

Later Years & Death Orwell battled tuberculosis, a disease that ultimately caused his death on 21 January 1950 in London at age 46 Wikipediafactsking.com. Despite his relatively short life, his body of work—novels, essays, journalism—leaves a lasting impact on literature and political thought.

Recent Recognition A new documentary, Orwell: 2+2=5, premiered recently at Cannes, highlighting Orwell’s prescience about manipulation and surveillance, and his enduring relevance in today's political climate TIME.
