The Emperor of All Maladies
The Emperor of All Maladies: A Biography of Cancer by Siddhartha Mukherjee is a Pulitzer Prize-winning book published in 2010. It traces the history of cancer from its earliest documented cases to modern treatments. Mukherjee, an oncologist, blends medical science with patient stories and personal reflections. The book explores the evolution of cancer research, therapies, and public perception. A powerful narrative, it humanizes cancer while providing deep scientific insight.

Oxygen Advantages
The Oxygen Advantage by Patrick McKeown teaches that most people over-breathe, reducing oxygen efficiency and harming health. It emphasizes nasal, light, and slow breathing to improve oxygen delivery using the Bohr Effect. Key tools like the BOLT score help track breathing efficiency and progress. Breath-holding and simulated altitude training improve endurance, focus, and red blood cell production. The book links better breathing to reduced anxiety, asthma relief, improved sleep, and athletic performance. Its methods are practical, science-based, and suitable for both athletes and everyday people.

Blood
The Science, Medicine, and Mythology of Menstruation by Dr. Jen Gunter was published in January 2024. It aims to debunk myths and stigma around menstruation using clear, evidence-based science. Dr. Gunter explains the biology of periods, hormonal cycles, and common menstrual disorders. She addresses topics like PMS, PCOS, endometriosis, period products, and misinformation online. The book challenges the cultural shame around menstruation and criticizes patriarchal health narratives. Written in a bold, witty style, it empowers readers to understand and advocate for their own health.

When Breath Becomes Air
When Breath Becomes Air is a memoir by Dr. Paul Kalanithi, published in 2016 after his death from lung cancer. The book chronicles his journey from literature student to neurosurgeon to terminally ill patient. It explores themes of mortality, purpose, and what makes life meaningful. Kalanithi reflects on the doctor-patient relationship from both sides. The writing is philosophical, emotional, and deeply personal. An epilogue by his wife Lucy adds closure and honors his legacy.

Breath
The book highlights the importance of nasal breathing, slow and deep breaths for better health. Nestor combines ancient breathing practices with modern scientific research. He shares personal experiments showing how changing breathing habits improves sleep, anxiety, and athletic performance. The book reveals connections between breathing and conditions like asthma and sleep apnea. Written in an engaging style, it’s inspired a global resurgence of interest in breathwork.

Jaws
The Story of a Hidden Epidemic by Sandra Kahn and Paul R. Ehrlich reveals how modern habits like soft diets and mouth breathing have led to underdeveloped jaws and widespread health issues. The book explains that poor jaw growth contributes to crooked teeth, sleep apnea, bad posture, and even impaired brain function. It highlights the evolutionary mismatch between our current environment and the natural needs of jaw development.Instead, they advocate for natural solutions like proper chewing, nasal breathing, and correct tongue posture. Blending science and clinical insight, the book calls for early, preventive action to combat this "hidden epidemic."

Why We Sleep
Neuroscientist and sleep expert Matthew Walker provides a revolutionary exploration of sleep, examining how it affects every aspect of our physical and mental well-being. Charting the most cutting-edge scientific breakthroughs, and marshalling his decades of research and clinical practice, Walker explains how we can harness sleep to improve learning, mood and energy levels, regulate hormones, prevent cancer, Alzheimer's and diabetes, slow the effects of aging, and increase longevity. He also provides actionable steps towards getting a better night's sleep every night.

Good Energy
The book explains how good metabolic health—“Good Energy”-is key to preventing chronic diseases. It highlights six eating principles focusing on whole foods, balanced nutrients, and meal timing. Regular movement and varied exercise support energy production and overall health. Quality sleep and aligning with natural circadian rhythms are crucial for metabolic function. Managing stress and cultivating a fearless, positive mindset improve mental and physical well-being. A practical four-week plan helps readers apply these habits for lasting vitality and health.

Blind Spots
In Blind Spots, Dr. Marty Makary examines how systemic issues in modern medicine—such as groupthink, bias, and resistance to new evidence—have led to public health missteps. He highlights examples like the opioid crisis, the peanut allergy epidemic, and the overuse of antibiotics to illustrate how medical dogma can hinder progress. Makary advocates for a more transparent, evidence-based approach to healthcare, encouraging both professionals and patients to question established norms and seek out the underlying science behind medical recommendations.

Follow the Science
In Follow the Science, Sharyl Attkisson delves into the intricate relationships between the pharmaceutical industry, government agencies, and the media. She examines how these entities often prioritize profits over public health, leading to misinformation and compromised medical practices. Attkisson presents case studies and investigative findings to highlight instances where scientific data has been manipulated or suppressed, urging readers to critically assess the information presented as "scientific consensus."
