Amitav Ghosh – Life, Work & Legacy

Early Life & Academic Background

• Born: 11 July 1956, Calcutta (now Kolkata).Grew up across India, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, and Iran, influenced by his diplomat father’s postings Encyclopedia BritannicaGradeSaver.

• Earned his BA (1976) and MA (1978) from the University of Delhi, followed by a PhD in Social Anthropology from the University of Oxford in 1982 Encyclopedia BritannicaGradeSaver.

Taught at institutions including Delhi University, the American University in Cairo, Columbia University, Queens College (CUNY), and Harvard (from 2004), before transitioning to full-time writing Encyclopedia BritannicaGradeSaver.

Fictional Works & Major Novels The Circle of Reason (1986): His debut novel, blending fable and picaresque elements; won France’s Prix Médicis (étranger) Encyclopedia Britannicaamitavghosh.comWikipedia. The Shadow Lines (1988): Family saga across generations and national boundaries; earned the Sahitya Akademi Award and Ananda Puraskar amitavghosh.comWikipedia.The Calcutta Chromosome (1995): A sci-fi-inflected novel exploring alternate histories of malaria’s discovery; won the Arthur C. The Glass Palace (2000): Pan-Asian historical epic; was withdrawn from the Commonwealth Writers' Prize in protest over the language criteria GradeSaverWikipedia. The Hungry Tide (2004): Set in the Sundarbans, focusing on marginalized lives amidst ecological vulnerabilities Encyclopedia BritannicaGradeSaver.

Amitav Ghosh — Ibis Trilogy

Novel Publication Year Highlights
Sea of Poppies 2008 Shortlisted for Man Booker; won Crossword and Golden Quill Awards, and the Tagore Literature Award
River of Smoke 2011 Shortlisted for the Man Asian Literary Prize
Flood of Fire 2015 Final volume of the trilogy
Gun Island (2019): A contemporary novel weaving myth, identity, and climate anxieties Encyclopedia Britannica. Jungle Nama (2021): A graphic verse adaptation of the Bon Bibi legend from the Sundarbans, illustrated by Salman Toor Wikipedia.

3. Nonfiction & Essays In an Antique Land (1992): Blends travel memoir, history, and cultural detective work in Egypt Encyclopedia Britannica. Other essay collections include Dancing in Cambodia, The Imam and the Indian, Incendiary Circumstances amitavghosh.com. The Great Derangement: Climate Change and the Unthinkable (2016): A powerful intervention on our cultural failure to narrate climate catastrophe Wikipedia.The Nutmeg’s Curse (2021): An ecological and colonial history of the Banda Islands, blending narrative and research Wikipedia. Smoke and Ashes: Opium’s Hidden Histories (2023): Historical account of the opium trade and colonial legacies Wikipedia.
